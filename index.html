UF's Co-Ed National Service Fraternity built on Leadership, Friendship, and Service. We welcome students of all majors and years to earn volunteer hours, gain leadership experience, and make lifelong friendships.

Who We Are

We host weekly service and fellowship events that give our members the opportunity to make a consistent impact both on campus and in the Gainesville community. Our chapter partners with a variety of local non-profits to support causes such as sustainability, education, and community wellness. Some of our regular partners include the Repurpose Project, North Florida Sewing Center, and the Giving Garden, where we assist with everything from organizing donations to hands-on volunteer work.

On campus, we are best known for operating the Information Booth near Turlington Hall, a long-standing tradition that allows us to help fellow students navigate UF’s campus and share important university updates. In addition to our weekly service, we also participate in special projects with organizations like the Lubee Bat Conservancy, Jungle Friends Primate Sanctuary, and other unique animal sanctuaries, allowing members to engage in hands-on conservation and animal care efforts.

Through these diverse opportunities, our members develop leadership skills, build lasting friendships, and contribute meaningfully to our community while living out the values of Alpha Phi Omega: Leadership, Friendship, and Service.

Our Values: Leadership, Friendship, and Service

Leadership We believe that everyone has the potential to lead. Whether you're stepping into a formal role or taking initiative behind the scenes, APO provides countless opportunities to develop leadership skills that make a lasting impact. From organizing service events and running meetings to mentoring Pledges and leading committees, our chapter empowers members to grow into confident, capable leaders, on campus and beyond. Many of our Brothers also represent APO at regional and national conferences, gaining even broader leadership exposure.

Friendship Our Brotherhood is built on strong, meaningful connections. Friendship in APO goes beyond just hanging out, it's about supporting each other through service, celebrating milestones, and building a community that feels like home. Through weekly fellowship events, retreats, and shared experiences, we form lasting bonds that extend far beyond our time at UF. Our inclusive and co-ed environment creates space for people from all walks of life to find belonging.

Service Service is the heart of our mission. We are committed to making a positive difference in our campus, community, and nation. Our chapter hosts weekly service projects and collaborates with local organizations such as the Repurpose Project, Giving Garden, North Florida Sewing Center, Ronald McDonald House, GRACE Marketplace, Jungle Friends Primate Sanctuary, and more. Whether we’re planting gardens, serving meals, or supporting animal sanctuaries, we show up with purpose and compassion.

Together, Leadership, Friendship, and Service define what it means to be a Brother of Alpha Phi Omega. They guide our actions, unite our members, and inspire us to create meaningful change, every day.

Our Services

Service is at the core of who we are. Our chapter hosts weekly service events that allow members to give back to the Gainesville community in diverse and impactful ways. We partner with a wide range of local organizations to provide hands-on support where it's needed most. Some of our ongoing service partners and projects include:

Repurpose Project – assisting with sustainability initiatives and donation sorting

North Florida Sewing Center – supporting programs that teach practical skills to underserved communities

Giving Garden – helping with planting, harvesting, and garden maintenance to promote food access

Lubee Bat Conservancy – assisting in bat habitat care and conservation education

Jungle Friends Primate Sanctuary – working directly with rescued monkeys and sanctuary upkeep

GRACE Marketplace – serving meals and supporting Gainesville’s unhoused community

Peaceful Paths – collecting donations and supporting survivors of domestic violence

Alachua County Humane Society – volunteering at adoption events and supporting animal care

Project Downtown Gainesville – distributing meals and hygiene supplies to individuals experiencing homelessness

And many more – our service calendar is always growing to reflect the evolving needs of our community
