Who We Are

We are the Tau Chapter of Alpha Phi Omega at the University of Florida. Our members work with local organizations like the Repurpose Project, North Florida Sewing Center, Giving Garden, and more. Alpha Phi Omega is a national co-ed service fraternity founded on the principles of leadership, friendship, and service. At the University of Florida, our Tau Chapter provides opportunities for students of all majors and years to gain volunteer hours, build leadership experience, and form lasting friendships through service to our campus and community.

Our Values

Through service events, leadership opportunities, and fellowship, we aim to foster strong connections and make a meaningful impact on campus and in the Gainesville community.

What We Do

We organize weekly service and fellowship events, partner with local non-profits, and run the campus information booth near Turlington Hall. We also participate in special projects with Lubee Bat Conservancy, Jungle Friends Primate Sanctuary, and more.

How to Join

Interested in pledging? Attend one of our Rush events and info meetings during the first two weeks of each semester. Learn more by following us on Instagram or checking our upcoming events!
